Sweden has consistently produced some top-notch ice hockey players, and Hampus Lindholm is one of them. 

Lindholm was born on January 20, 1994, and is currently a defenceman for the Boston Bruins of the National Hockey League (NHL).

He started his amateur ice hockey career with the Jonstorps U20 team in the 2011-12 season. Then, in the next season, he joined the Rögle BK U20.

This was only the beginning, as the Swed had a fruitful career lined up in front of him.

Lindholm started his professional career with Rögle and was crowned the U20 Super Elit League’s Best Defenseman.

The Anaheim Ducks selected him sixth overall in the 2012 NHL Entry Draft. Furthermore, he even had the opportunity to represent his country internationally.

Lindholm competed for Sweden in the 2012 U18 World Junior Championship.
