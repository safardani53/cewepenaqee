Mr. T, 67, looks far from his A-Team days as he steps out in sweatpants and a gray beard The US

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Sunday, February 4, 2024

Mr. T, 67, looked far from his A-Team days as he stepped out in sweatpants and a gray beard.

The actor went on an errands run, checking his mail and picking up groceries.

“Mr. T” - known for A-Team television series fame - was spotted coming out from coronavirus quarantining to run errands near his Los Angeles home.

The 67-year-old actor's real name is Lawrence Tureaud.

He went grocery shopping and then checked his mail using a piece of cloth to open the box in order to avoid contact with germs.

He was wearing stained workout pants, an orange beanie and a long sleeve blue T-shirt.

Mr. T also appeared to have let his beard grow out while quarantining.

Mr. T recently took to Twitter to thank all the healthcare workers fighting the pandemic.

He wrote: "To All the Great Doctors and Nurses fighting coronavirus around the world, This is my prayer from God to you.

"First, Thank you, Thank you, Thank you, Thank you for your unwavering sacrifice & Thank you for your Tireless Dedication..."

He was diagnosed with cancer back in 1995 but made a full recovery.

Mr. T first started out in the business as a celebrity bodyguard for names like Muhammad Ali, Steve McQueen and Michael Jackson.

He rose to fame in Rocky III opposite Sylvester Stallone as boxer Clubber Lang.

But he most well remembered for his role on The A-Team.

He played former special forces operative turned fugitive Bosco Albert "B.A" Baracus from 1983-1987.

The role made him a pop culture icon, and in 1983 First Lady Nancy Reagan notably sat on his knee when he posed as Santa Claus at the White House.
