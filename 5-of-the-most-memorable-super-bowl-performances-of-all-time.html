5 Of The Most Memorable Super Bowl Performances Of All Time

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Friday, August 16, 2024

The Super Bowl is a larger-than-life sports event that has included legendary performers throughout the years. These performances have aided the NFL’s growth in popularity, especially in countries where the sport is not as well-known. In this section, we will discuss five such classic performances that not only shook the stage but also captured everyone’s hearts.

Five unforgettable Super Bowl halftime shows

1) Michael Jackson (first) (1993)

In 1993, the King of Pop gave one of the most unforgettable Super Bowl performances. Michael Jackson delivered a brief set that featured classics such as We Are the World, Heal the World, and Billie Jean. Those who were fortunate enough to see the musical phenomenon live were also treated to his iconic moonwalk.

2) U2’s (2002)

NFL fans had the opportunity to see U2, the legendary Irish rock band from Dublin, play lived in the Louisiana Superdome in New Orleans in 2002. They played a brief but unforgettable setlist, paying respect to those who died in the terrible 9/11 attack. Their set selection included chart-topping hits including Beautiful Day, MLK, and Where the Streets Have No Name.

3) The Rolling Stones  (2006)

During the Super Bowl XL Halftime Show in 2006, the legendary British rock band The Rolling Stones delivered an incredible performance at Ford Field in Detroit, Michigan. The supergroup’s stage was designed in the shape of their emblem. Their brief set selection included hits including Start Me Up, Rough Justice, and Satisfaction.

4) Shakira and Jennifer Lopez (2020)

It’s not often that two music business megastars, such as Shakira and Jennifer Lopez, play on the same stage. This, however, is precisely what occurred at the 2020 edition of this huge sports event.

The pair delivered an incredible performance that drew one of the greatest applause from the audience in Super Bowl history. Emme, Lopez’s daughter, also appeared on the program and played Bruce Springsteen’s hit song Born in the U.S.A.

5) Prince (2007)

The roof of the Hard Rock Stadium in Florida rocked to the amazing performance by the American musical superstar Prince during Super Bowl XLI. The megastar was seen playing a mix of Queen’s We Will Rock You and the Foo Fighters’ Best of You.
