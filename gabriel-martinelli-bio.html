Gabriel Martinelli is a Brazilian soccer player who plays for the Premier League club Arsenal as a forward or left-winger. He has been showering magic on the soccer field with his incredible skills at a very young age and inspiring youth worldwide.

On 5 August 2022, Martinelli became the first Brazilian to net a season-opening goal of the Premier League campaign by scoring for Arsenal in their 2-0 away win at Crystal Palace.

Moreover, his potential is mind-boggling; however, one must work hard and stay focused to achieve that.

The talented player has those qualities in abundance, making him one of this generation’s top young prospects.

Gabriel Martinelli | Early Life 

As a child, Gabriel struggled with basic needs as his family was destitute. Consequently, they lived in one of the most deprived areas in the city, where a crime was rife.

Hence, his parents wanted young Martinelli to stay away from criminal activities. So, with that in mind, his father introduced Gabriel to soccer at a very young age.

He got so addicted to soccer that he lost interest in his education.

Seeing this, Joao Martinelli, his father, encouraged him to play soccer. Like every father wants his child to be successful, Martinelli Sr. wants his son to be the best.

Subsequently, Martinelli skilled his soccer through futsal games. Having tricky feet and raw pace helped propel the Arsenal forward through the ranks and become the top talent of his age group.

Gabriel Martinelli | Early Career

Martinelli would perform weekly for his futsal team until the Corinthians Futsal team saw his talent and snapped him up at nine. Upon arriving, young Gabriel impressed many people, including his coach.

After honing and developing his futsal skills, the U-23 Brazil international decided to make soccer his priority. The transition to soccer was flawless as the diminutive forward scored goals for fun.

Due to his raw ability and skills, Ituano FC came swooping in for the young forward.

His fourth-division Brazilian performances were so impressive that many top European clubs wanted to take Martinelli on trial.

Manchester United and Barcelona showed the most interest in the Brazilian-born forward. Subsequently, he went on trials with the above clubs without making any impact.

As a result, the Arsenal attacker had to move back to Brazil to further his soccer development.

He remained with Ituano FC until his big move to Arsenal in 2019 for $7.10 million. It was a life-changing point for young Martinelli.

Gabriel Martinelli | Professional Career 

Martinelli’s professional career started with Ituano when he made his debut at 16 years and nine months. In doing, so, he became the club’s youngest player to debut in this century.

Martinelli earned a spot in the 2019 Campeonato Paulista after his impressive performances. Gabriel scored six goals during the competition, true to his form, helping his team reach the quarter-finals.

As a result, the Arsenal forward earned himself a coveted spot in the pre-Copa America training camp for the Brazilian national team in May 2019.

It was as if the 18-year-old was dreaming because he got the opportunity to play with superstars like Neymar and Coutinho, among others.

He gained much-needed experience after rubbing shoulders with some of the best in the world.

Subsequently, Gabriel won Campeonato Paulista Best Newcomer, Countryside Player of the Year, and Team of the Year 2019 in the same year.

Clubs all around Europe had taken note of Martinelli’s performances and wanted to sign him. But it was Arsenal that managed to secure his services.

Arsenal players welcomed the Brazil U-23 international in North London like a superstar who made him feel special and wanted.

As a result, he signed for Arsenal. Gabriel fulfilled his lifelong dream of playing in the Premier League against the players he used to watch on television and PlayStation.

It seems like Gabriel has nerves of steel because, on his first competitive start for the Gunners, he scored a brace in a 5-0 win.

Following his impressive performance, Unai Emery, Arsenal’s coach at the time, said that Martinelli is a fighter who loves to help his teammates. 

Similarly, he is showing magic on the soccer field.

Further

Jurgen Klopp, the Liverpool manager, labeled Martinelli “the talent of the century.”

To illustrate, Gabriel scored seven goals in seven appearances. A feat that the likes of Messi and Ronaldo would be proud of.

Gabriel won Arsenal’s Player of the Month award for October due to his outstanding performance for the club.

However, his best moment for the Gunners has to be scoring against their London rival, Chelsea.

In displaying his raw speed and finishing ability, the Guarulhos-born attacker managed to skip past Ngolo Kante and sprinted another 67-yards before calmly slotting the ball past a helpless Kepa Arrizabalga.

He became the first teenager to reach double figures for the club since Nikolas Anelka.

At the time of writing, Martinelli has scored ten goals in 24 appearances for the North London outfit.

He has won English FA Cup and the English Super Cup, also he is also an Olympic medalist.

On June 21 2020, he had surgery to repair a lesion in the cartilage of his knee following an injury. He returned on 19 December, coming off the bench to play 19 minutes in a 2-1 away defeat to Everton.

During the practice match against Newcastle United in the FA Cup on 9 January 2021, he had a minor ankle injury and was substituted by Reis Nelson.

He scored twice against Leeds United on 18 December, marking his first Premier League brace, with his first goal marking Arsenal’s 7000th scored in top-division English soccer.

Martinelli was withdrawn from the match for two yellow cards in four seconds against wolves on 10 February 2022. He received his career’s first red card.

The red card gained some controversy because he was given two yellow cards in one passage of play, starting with pushing Daniel Podence after a throw-in and then a foul against Nelson Semedo.

Gabriel Martinelli | International Career

Since he was born in Brazil, and his father is Italian, Martinelli had the privilege of choosing his nationality between two international giants soccer playing countries. He holds dual Brazilian-Italian citizenship.

Any team will demand a talented player like him to improve the team’s strength. Therefore, it should be no surprise that Brazil and Italy are fighting over the Arsenal player.

Brazil won the race as Gabriel appeared for the Brazil U-23 at Spain’s United International Football Festival. He helped Brazil win an Olympic Gold in men’s soccer at the 2020 Summer Olympics.

He can always change his decision; however he likes.

Just ask Diego Costa, the Atletico Madrid striker who declined the opportunity to play for Brazil at the 2014 FIFA World Cup in Brazil. Instead, he chose the Spanish national team for whom he currently plays.
