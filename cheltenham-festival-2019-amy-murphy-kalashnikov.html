Cheltenham Festival 2019: Amy Murphy has last minute call to make with stable star Kalashnikov ahead

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Sunday, July 14, 2024

AMY MURPHY has one of the most important decisions of her career to make in the build up to Cheltenham Festival.

Stable star Kalashnikov was always on course for the Arkle, but recent events have meant the JLT is also under serious consideration giving Murphy a high-class headache.

He made a foot-perfect start to life as a chaser - notching up back-to-back wins at Warwick and Plumpton.

The Supreme runner-up was then beat by Dynamite Dollars at Kempton, before his more recent defeat to the improving Glen Forsa.

Murphy said: "Don’t get me wrong, Sandown was obviously very disappointing – we wanted to go there and put up a great show.

"I think it’s been very well documented that he’s not very good right-handed, I’m looking forward to getting him back to where he ran a career best at the Festival. Hopefully, he’ll come back to his own.

"He barely blew after the race, so for whatever reason, he didn’t let himself down. It’s one of those that in a couple of months’ time, we can look back and just put a line through it.

"He’s done it before at Sandown, it was a blow-by-blow effort from him in the Tolworth (Hurdle) and then he went on and won a Betfair. At this point, I’m just trying to move on from it and move forward."

After an agonising second to Summerville Boy in the Supreme at last year's Festival, Murphy is hoping to go one better with her flag-bearer this time around.

The Newmarket handler continued: "We’ll keep both options open. We’ll keep both the Arkle and JLT in mind. I’m not tied down to one at the moment, but we’re trying to keep a similar path.

"He’ll go back to his Supreme, his Betfair and his novice chases, he’s no slouch and he’s not slow.

"The Arkle is very much at the back of our minds because you do have to stay all day to win an Arkle.

"We’ll see how we go in the next couple of weeks, the ground will be a factor, and who’s going where will be a factor.

"It’ll be a last minute decision."
