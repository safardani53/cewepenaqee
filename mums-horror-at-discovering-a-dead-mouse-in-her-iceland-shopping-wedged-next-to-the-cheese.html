Mum's horror at discovering a dead MOUSE in her Iceland shopping wedged next to the cheese

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Sunday, February 18, 2024

A MUM is disgusted and claims she'll never shop at Iceland again after finding a DEAD MOUSE in her home delivery order.

Hannah Alexander ordered her shopping online from the frozen food chain's store in Islington, North London, on Monday night, organising for it to arrive for when she got home from work the following day.

But when the bulk order was delivered, five-year-old son Malachi made the shocking discovery.

Speaking to The Sun Online, the mum of two said: "We'd unpacked most of the bag when my little boy put his hand in and said 'mum, there's a mouse in there'.

"I thought he was joking.

"It's surreal. It was wedged between my bananas and cheese. I couldn't believe there was a mouse in my house."

Single-mum Hannah, who also has a 15-year-old daughter Madison, claims she was in the same store eight months ago when she saw another mouse running up and down an aisle and filmed it on her mobile phone.

When she confronted the store she says they told her they had dealt with it.

The education consultant said: "There were still loads of bags all over my floor, waiting to be unpacked, I refused to look I was so worried I'd find another one."

Around three hours later Iceland returned to collect the shopping and offered Hannah a refund.

The mum, who isn't a regular Iceland shopper, added: "As a consumer I just feel people need to know about this. My shopping was contaminated so how many other people's was too?

"It was quite a long time before they came to pick it up. I kept having visions of the mouse thawing out and running around the room."

A spokeswoman for Iceland said: "We take all complaints extremely seriously. We can confirm that Ms Alexander has been in touch with the store regarding a mouse in her home delivery shopping and our Customer Care team is contacting her about this matter.

"We are conducting a full investigation and have arranged for Ms Alexander to receive a full refund as a gesture of good will."
