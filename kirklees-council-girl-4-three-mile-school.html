Girl, 4, told by Kirklees Council to travel THREE miles to school every day despite living just 900

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Friday, April 12, 2024

A FOUR-YEAR-OLD girl has been told she must travel three miles to school instead of just 900m after being told the closest schools were too full.

Little Arriannah Hussein was given a spot at Linthwaite Clough JI School, missing out on her family's top three preferences closer to their Meltham home in West Yorkshire.

Mum-of-three Zoey Atkins has now said her school run will be "near impossible" as she faces trying to drop off her children to different schools before making it to her university lectures on time.

Speaking to the Huddersfield Daily Examiner, she said: "I am a single mother of three, attending a full-time university degree. As my youngest child will be continuing his nursery place at Honley Daycare, the school is in the total opposite direction.

"It will be a near impossible for me to drop my son off at his daycare, my daughter off at school in Linthwaite and be in my university lecture for 9am-9.30am in the morning."

Her first choice for little Arriannah  was Meltham Moor Primary School, with her second choice Meltham C of E Primary School - both within 900m from her home.

The family's third choice was Brokholes - which mum Zoey chose so that she could drop Arriannah off minutes away from her youngest son who attends nearby Honley Daycare.

However, instead, Arriannah was handed a place at Linthwaite Clough JI - three miles from the family home and nowhere near her son's school.

Labelling the decision as "extremely upsetting", she said she was aware of other families in a similar situation - with huge housing developments now going up in Linthwaitre and putting pressure on future school places.

A spokesman for Kirklees Council said: "When a school is over-subscribed certain published criteria are used to allocate the available places.

"The places at Miss Akins’ three preferred schools – all of which were over-subscribed – have been offered to families in line with the criteria or who live nearer to them.

"However Miss Akins was offered a place at the nearest school with available places and we are also helping her to look at different options. These include other schools which may be more convenient, while we will also advise on how to appeal for a place at the preferred schools."

They said that just over 97 per cent of families had been allocated a place at one of their preferred schools.

It's not the first time that families have been left frustrated at trying to get their children to school - with a mum filming the muddy three-mile walk that her nine-year-old daughter faced everyday.

Parents have also clashed with enforcement officers over drop-off zones outside a school.
