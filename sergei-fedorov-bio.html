Sergei Fedorov: Early Life 

Ever since Sergei was a child, he dreamed of becoming a professional football player as Europe was heavily influenced by football.

But, when his parents, Viktor Fedorov and Natalia Fedorov, decided to move to the northern part of the USSR, his priorities changed from football to ice hockey.

The reason was that part of the country had winter for nine months, which meant that it was virtually impossible to practice outside. As a result, young Sergei decided to play ice hockey instead.

Subsequently, the future Hall of Famer honed his skills as Fedorov would spend all of his time practicing and developing his talents whenever he was not in school.

And, the rest is history because the USSR-born player turned out to be probably the best export of the USSR to the NHL.

Sergei Fedorov: Career & Stats

Before his NHL career, Fedorov was playing in the Soviet Union for the club he is managing now, CSKA Moscow.

But, in a stroke of luck, Sergei got to show his talents to the American audience when CSKA Moscow played in the pre-1990 Goodwill Games in Portland, Oregon.

What followed after that is the stuff of legends as the Detroit Red Wings drafted the USSR-born player 74th overall in the 1989 NHL Entry Draft.

At the moment, no one would have imagined the impact Sergei would have not only for Detroit Red Wings but the NHL as a whole.

To illustrate, the 6-feet-2 center played for the Red Wings in a grand total of 1070 games.

And in those games, he scored 481 goals, provided 588 assists to his teammates, and scored 1182 points.

Also, the Hall of Famer won three Stanley Cups with the Red Wings in the years 1997, 1998, and 2002 as part of the “Russian Five” that included Viacheslav Fetisov, Igor Larionov, Vladimir Konstantinov, and Vyacheslav Kozlov.

Besides, Sergei was also named the winner of the “oldest and most prestigious individual award in hockey,” the Hart Memorial Trophy, which is awarded to the NHL’s Most Valuable Player in 1994.

Also, Fedorov became a six-time All-Star with the Red Wings for his outstanding consistent service over the course of his 13 years of stay in Detroit.

However, the 1994 NHL MVP did not just play for the Red Wings but also played for three other NFL teams.

For instance, Fedorov played for the Mighty Duck of Anaheim, Columbus Blue Jackets, and Washington Capitals.

At the time of his retirement from the NHL, Sergei had played in an incredible 1431 games in which he scored 535 goals, assisted 820 times, and scored a further 1355 points.

Thus, on 9 November 2015, Fedorov was inducted into the Hockey Hall of Fame for his outstanding contributions to the NHL.

International Career

Since the very beginning of the sport, USSR (now Russia) has always been the best along with the U.S.

Therefore, it should come as no surprise that Fedorov has won just as much with his country as he did with the Red Wings.

To illustrate, Sergei was part of the Soviet team that won the World Championships gold for two consecutive years in 1989 and 1990.

The 6-feet-2 center won the silver medal in 1988 and gold in the 1989 World Junior Championships.

Furthermore, the six-time NHL All-Star played in the 1998 Olympics in Nagano and the 2002 Olympics in the U.S, where he won silver and bronze medals.

Similarly, Fedorov has played two times in the World Championships, winning the gold in 2008 and silver in 2010.

And, finally, the Hall of Famer appeared once in the World Cup of Hockey during the year 1996, in which he won a bronze medal.

In conclusion, Sergei was one of the best players in the USSR and the whole world, as suggested by his impressive haul of achievements.

In fact, only a handful of athletes, let alone ice hockey players boast the awards and recognition that Fedorov has garnered throughout his astonishing 26 years of playing career.
