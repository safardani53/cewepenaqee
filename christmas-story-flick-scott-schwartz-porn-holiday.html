A Christmas Story's Scott Schwartz starred in PORN years after he iconically stuck his tongue to pol

Posted by Martina Birk on Wednesday, April 17, 2024

A CHRISTMAS Story's Scott Schwartz starred in adult film porn roles after his iconic performance as Flick in the scene where he got his tongue stuck to the frozen pole.

Years after his memorable "double-dog-dare" scene, Scott went on to perform in non-sexual roles in adult films, managed porn actors, and after the millennium continued to rack up IMDB credits acting in small roles in films as he runs a collectibles store with his dad.

Scott, now 53, made his break as a child actor after being cast in a commercial.

He then starred in the 1982 movie The Toy next to actor and comedian Richard Pryor before landing his iconic role as Flick in A Christmas Story.

The 1983 cult classic directed by Bob Clark wouldn't have been the same without Scott - aka Flick - being put up to a "double-dog-dare" from the town bullies.

Ralphie - played by Peter Billingsley, 50 - stared open-mouthed at Flick as he struggled to get his tongue unstuck from a frozen pole.

Scott continued to act in children's roles until his adolescence, when he discovered a career as an actor was something not everyone is easily handed.

The A Christmas Story star opened up to the independent culture blogger Jerry Saravia about his struggles in the unglamorous industry: "Being a child actor, while rewarding, is truly a bad job.

"We all grow up and if the right people don't take to us and keep us working, most are out of work by 15 to 18 years old and have no idea what the real world is about."

Turning to work in the adult film industry wasn't Scott's first choice.

He shared: "It came down to dollars and cents. It was paying my bills and it was whatever it took to take care of putting a roof over my head and food on the table."

Scott reported: "In the adult film industry, I worked for a talent agency, a production office, as a video salesman. About any job you can think of, I did."

Over his time in the industry, Scott also appeared onscreen in the adult films, but never in any sexual rolls.

One film was even titled after himself: Scotty's X-Rated Adventure.

Scott allegedly won an AVN Award - the industry equivalent to an Oscar - in 1997 for Best Non-Sexual Performance in Silver Screen Confidential.

After representing porn stars as an industry agent, Scott had enough of the field and left to continue to pursue acting in small roles in mainstream movies.

Since the early 2000s, Scott continued to act in movies acquiring small roles in Skin Walker, Unseen Evil 2, and Community College.

Over the pandemic, Scott played himself in a comedic television series called The Quarantine Bunch which followed a group of former child actors Zooming into their weekly support group.

Scott has had a passion for supporting youth actors in following their dreams, as he held a fundraising gala for the charity A Minor Consideration, a non-profit foundation providing support for child performers.

Along with his return to acting on television and in movies, Scott runs a vintage memorabilia store in California with his dad and has hopes to write a book delving more into his qualms with his child acting career.
