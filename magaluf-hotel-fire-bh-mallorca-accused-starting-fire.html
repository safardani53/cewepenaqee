What caused the Magaluf hotel fire, where is the BH Mallorca and who's been accused of starting the

Posted by Martina Birk on Sunday, July 7, 2024

A MAGALUF hotel fire has led to the arrest of British tourists as part of an investigation into what started the blaze.

Here is what we know so far about the incident in the party resort.

What caused the Magaluf hotel fire?

Police in the Spanish resort are investigating whether the fire at the BH Mallorca Hotel was accidently started by a prank gone wrong.

They said they believed four British holidaymakers set light to the room after trying to wake up a pal who was asleep on the bed by burning his feet using an aerosol and a lighter.

The Brits have been photographed after appearing in court in in the Majorcan capital Palma.

Where is the BH Mallorca?

BH Mallorca hotel is near the infamous night zones of Magaluf in an area popular with British, Irish and German partygoers.

It costs about Â£50 a night at this time of year and boasts pool parties and water slides.

According to the Civil Guard 100 guests were evacuated.

No-one was badly hurt in the drama, although several people including three Civil Guard officers had to be treated for smoke inhalation.

Firefighters, who used extendable ladders to bring several holidaymakers to safety, have been praised for their speedy actions.

The fire destroyed only one room.

Who's been accused of starting the blaze?

Respected local paper Diario de Mallorca said four Brits were arrested.

They have been named locally as Brandon McCahill, 21; James Brown, 21; Lauren Smith, 19; and Georgia James, also 19.

It was reported they claimed they were on the balcony smoking, chatting and drinking when the room began to burn.
