Career

Sono began his career as a poet before moving on to film filmmaking, creating a series of short films on Super 8 as a student. Later, in 1986, he debuted as a director, writer, and actor in the film A Man’s Flower Road. He has since appeared in films such as Decisive Match! Despite having a career spanning over four decades, he rose to international prominence in 2008 with his film Love Exposure. It’s a nearly four-hour video about an up-the-skirt photographer’s sensual escapades and spiritual journey. The film debuted at the 2009 Berlin Film Festival and went on to win several accolades. Sono fled away from home to join a cult, which subsequently inspired him to become a filmmaker. Love Exposure, his 2008 film, was inspired by his own life experience.
