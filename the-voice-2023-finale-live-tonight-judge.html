Past winners of The Voice

The Voice is one of the most watched programs on TV, airing since 2011. 

It has completed 22 seasons since.

Below is a list of all the winners during the series,

  • Season 1 – Javier Colon (Team Adam)
  • Season 2 – Jermaine Paul (Team Blake)
  • Season 3 – Cassadee Pope (Team Blake)
  • Season 4 – Danielle Bradbery (Team Blake)
  • Season 5 – Tessanne Chin (Team Adam)
  • Season 6 – Josh Kaufman (Team Usher)
  • Season 7 – Craig Wayne Boyd (Team Blake)
  • Season 8 – Sawyer Fredericks (Team Pharrell)

Blake Shelton ends his time on The Voice

Blake Shelton finished out his 12-year stint on The Voice with Season 23.

He debuted with the singing competition in 2011 in the show’s premiere and was a staple with the program until he announced his departure last fall saying it was time for him to step away.

“I’ve been wrestling with this for a while, and I’ve decided that it’s time for me to step away from The Voice after Season 23,” he explained at the time.

“This show has changed my life in every way for the better, and it will always feel like home to me. It’s been a hell of a ride over these 12 years of chair turns, and I want to thank everyone at The Voice from NBC, every producer, the writers, musicians, crew and catering people; you are the best.”

The Voice reveals Blake’s replacement

Reba McEntire has joined the cast of The Voice as a full-time judge for Season 24 in place of Blake Shelton this fall after previously appearing as a mega-mentor.

She will be joining returning judges John Legend, Niall Horan, and Gwen Stefani.

This is the first season of the show without Blake, who announced in October 2022 that he would not be returning to his long-standing role.

During the show’s first season, Reba served as a Battle Advisor to Team Blake.

Chance the Rapper speaks on mental health

Sorelle closed out their time on The Voice by singing with coach, Chance the Rapper.

The four performed O-o-h Child by The Five Stairsteps.

Before they began, the rapper addressed the audience and said, "America, we want to remind y’all at home that even if you’re going through some things, sometimes it’s okay to not be okay,”
